Noise reduction

Definition

Noise reduction is a digital image processing technique used to reduce unwanted artifacts or disturbances in an image, often referred to as "noise." Noise can manifest as random variations in brightness or color in an image and can result from various sources such as electronic sensor limitations, low light conditions, or transmission errors. Reducing noise can enhance the visual quality and clarity of an image.

2. Noise Characterization

Before applying noise reduction, it's essential to understand the type of noise present in the image. Common types of noise include Gaussian noise, salt-and-pepper noise, and speckle noise. Knowing the noise characteristics helps in selecting the appropriate noise reduction technique.

Bilateral Filtering ::

A more sophisticated technique that preserves edges while smoothing noise.

b. Frequency Domain Filtering

Techniques like Fourier Transform can be used to convert the image into the frequency domain. Noise can then be filtered out in the frequency domain, and the inverse transform can be applied to obtain the noise-reduced image.

c. Deep Learning

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) can be trained to learn and remove noise from images. Denoising autoencoders and various pre-trained models are available for this purpose.
